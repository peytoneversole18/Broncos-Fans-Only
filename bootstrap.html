Broncos 2026 Draft Recap: (Accordion)

The Denver Broncos entered the 2026 NFL Draft without first- or second-round picks, making their first selection in the third round where they drafted Tyler Onyedim, a defensive lineman from Texas A&M. In the fourth round, they added running back Jonah Coleman from Washington and offensive lineman Kage Casey from Boise State to strengthen both sides of the offense. The Broncos continued in the fifth round by selecting tight end Justin Joly out of NC State. They wrapped up their draft in the seventh round with three picks, choosing safety Miles Scott from Illinois, tight end Dallen Bentley from Utah, and linebacker Red Murdock from Buffalo, rounding out a class focused on depth and versatility.
